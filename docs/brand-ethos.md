# PRISM Brand Ethos

## Core Belief

> **You are the light. Prism reveals the spectrum.**

PRISM does not position the system, model, or bot as the source of creativity.
The person arrives with the original light: intent, experience, taste,
curiosity, and judgment. PRISM is the instrument and creative sanctum where
that light can be protected, examined, refracted, and expressed.

The primary public slogan is:

> **One light. Many colors.**

The foundational truth behind it is:

> The prism does not create the colors. It reveals what was already inside the
> light.

## Meaning

| Element      | Meaning                                                      |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Source light | The person: their intent, imagination, history, and agency   |
| Prism        | The private creative sanctum and instrument                  |
| Refraction   | Exploration through tools, models, modes, and perspectives   |
| Colors       | Distinct voices, personas, forms, and creative possibilities |
| Spectrum     | The work and understanding that emerge through interplay     |

Colors suggest plurality; they are not a rigid taxonomy. A persona, mode, or
medium should never be permanently reduced to one brand color.

## Product Translation

Every experience should preserve these commitments:

- **The person remains the source.** PRISM can propose, challenge, combine, and
  illuminate, but it should not erase authorship or pretend that generated
  output arrived without human direction and judgment.
- **Multiplicity should expand agency.** Different bots and modes should reveal
  meaningful alternatives rather than add noise or fragment the workspace.
- **The sanctum should feel protected.** Local-first privacy, explicit online
  boundaries, calm presentation, and user-controlled memory are part of the
  creative experience—not merely infrastructure.
- **Interplay should matter.** The strongest result may come from colors meeting:
  personas responding to one another, tools changing the form of an idea, and
  the person deciding what belongs.
- **Utility and wonder belong together.** Practical clarity and experiential
  delight remain equally necessary. The metaphor must improve orientation and
  feeling without hiding controls or consequences.

## Mark System

PRISM uses three complementary marks rather than three competing logos:

1. **Refraction emblem — primary narrative mark.** One neutral ray enters the
   triangular prism and five P/R/I/S/M rays emerge. Use it for first contact,
   hero moments, onboarding, marketing, and prominent brand lockups.
2. **Wordmark — signature.** The five colored letterforms carry the product name
   and should accompany the emblem when space allows.
3. **Triangle — compact shorthand.** Use the hollow triangle for favicons, tiny
   controls, monochrome contexts, private/default avatar treatments, and other
   places where the full refraction story would become illegible.

The emblem was originally introduced as the Sandbox glyph. Its original
geometry, stroke weights, ray spacing, and P/R/I/S/M color order are promoted
unchanged; this is a reassignment, not a redraw. It now belongs to PRISM as a
whole and should not be treated as mode-specific. Its five output rays use the
wordmark palette in P/R/I/S/M order: rose, amber, lime, cyan, and violet.

## First-Time Creative Introduction

The eventual first-time introduction should express this ethos as an animated
cutscene and poem, beginning from the shared seed:

> “On the border between art and logic, there stood a colossal pyramid...”

That line is a creative seed, not a locked final script. The introduction
should preserve these narrative beats:

1. Establish the border between art and logic.
2. Reveal the colossal pyramid as a mysterious creative sanctum—not a machine
   that owns creativity.
3. Let the person arrive as the single light and cross the threshold.
4. Refract that light into voices, colors, media, and possible paths.
5. Show colors interacting so the spectrum feels emergent rather than sorted.
6. Return agency to the person with an invitation to enter PRISM.

The sequence should feel like a threshold ritual, not a feature tour. It must
remain skippable, replayable, respectful of reduced-motion preferences, and
separate from required technical setup. Skipping the poem must never block
entry, and setup should never interrupt its emotional arc.
